When Do PUC Admissions Start in 2026?

PUC admissions in Karnataka are typically tied to SSLC (Class 10) board examination results. The general timeline for 2026-27:

  • SSLC results announced: April–May 2026 (Karnataka Secondary Education Examination Board)
  • PUC admissions open: Immediately after SSLC results, typically May–June 2026
  • PUC classes begin: July 2026 (first day of academic year)
Act Early: Top PU colleges in Bengaluru fill seats within days of SSLC results. Enquire and register before results are announced to be first in line.

Documents Required for PUC Admission 2026-27

Prepare these documents before visiting any PU college for admission:

  • SSLC Marks Card — Original + 2 photocopies of your Class 10 result
  • SSLC Pass Certificate — Issued by KSEEB or your board
  • Transfer Certificate (TC) — From your Class 10 school, original required
  • Conduct Certificate — From previous school (some colleges require this)
  • Caste / Category Certificate — OBC, SC, ST certificates for reserved category students (original + copy)
  • Migration Certificate — Required for students from CBSE/ICSE/other state boards
  • Aadhaar Card — Student's Aadhaar for identity verification
  • Passport-Size Photographs — 4 to 6 recent photos
  • Parent/Guardian ID Proof — Aadhaar or voter ID of parent/guardian

    Pay the admission fee as specified by the college. Keep the receipt. Fees vary by college and stream; ask for the complete fee structure including tuition, lab fees, and any coaching charges.

    Collect your admission confirmation letter, college ID card application, and the academic schedule. Classes typically begin in July 2026.

How to Choose the Right PU College in Bengaluru

With hundreds of PU colleges in Bengaluru, here is how to narrow your choice:

  • Pass rate — Ask for the last 3 years' board exam results. A consistent 95%+ pass rate is a strong indicator.
  • Entrance exam results — For Science students, ask how many students qualified for NEET and CET in the last batch.
  • Faculty credentials — Experienced, subject-specialist teachers make a measurable difference.
  • Infrastructure — Visit labs and library in person. Properly equipped science labs are non-negotiable for PCMB/PCMC.
  • College recognition — Verify the college code with the PUE Department website to ensure it is recognised.
  • Location — A shorter commute means more study time. Choose a college within easy reach of your home.
